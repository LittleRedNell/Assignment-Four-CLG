Caring for your Australian Native Stingless Bees

Australian Native Stingless Bees are very self sufficient and once they are located in a suitable place in your yard or on your balcony, there is very little more that needs to be done. The main things to be aware of are summer heat (temperatures over 38oC) and predators. An ideal place for a native bee hive is in place that provides shade in summer and sun in winter. Bees will only be active at temperatures >18oC thus it is important to provide some sun in winter to ensure they have adequate time during the day for activity to collect stores1.

Remember: Summer heat, Winter Sun!

On hot summer days (38oC), bees may 'fan' at the entry to the hive. There are a few simple things you can do to prevent the bees overheating:

When selecting a site for your bees, ensure it is somewhere that will be in shade in summer.

If your bees are exhibiting signs the hive is hot (fanning at entry), place a wet towel over the top of the hive and/or you may drill a small (4mm) hole in the back of the top of the middle top/MIDDLE? section of the hive to help heat escape2.

Watch for Predators!

There are several predators which will attack native bee hives and can quickly result in the destruction and death of a hive. It is very important to familiarise yourself with these predators and check your hive regularly to ensure it is not being attacked.
